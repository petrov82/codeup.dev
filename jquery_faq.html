There is a lot of land here in the good ole US of A, and that means there is a lot of beauty and animal life people like to protect from those who don't like beauty. Have you heard of John Muir?

What can I find in a National Park?
Bears, mainly. Hungry, ravenous, agitated bears with sharp teeth and long claws. You know how they say that you shouldn't get between a bear and her cubs? In the National Parks, you will constantly find yourself in that position. Good Luck.
What about all that beauty you mentioned?
Oh, there is definitely beauty in each park. Mountains, streams, glacier–filled valleys (unless it's a global–warming summer), and all kinds of wildlife. But the bears—the bears are the gate–keepers
